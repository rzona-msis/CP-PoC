# User Personas - Campus Resource Hub

## Persona 1: Sarah - Graduate Student

**Demographics**:
- Age: 24
- Role: Graduate student in Data Science
- Department: Kelley School of Business

**Goals**:
- Find quiet study spaces for dissertation work
- Book conference rooms for group projects
- Access specialized software and equipment

**Pain Points**:
- Current booking systems are fragmented across departments
- Often arrives to find rooms double-booked
- No way to see reviews/ratings of spaces

**Tech Savviness**: High - comfortable with web apps and technology

**Usage Scenario**: 
Sarah needs a quiet study room for 3 hours tomorrow afternoon. She opens Campus Resource Hub, searches "study room quiet", filters by tomorrow's date and 2-5pm availability, views photos and ratings, and books her preferred room. She receives immediate confirmation.

## Persona 2: Dr. Martinez - Faculty / Staff

**Demographics**:
- Age: 42
- Role: Assistant Professor, Computer Science
- Manages: Lab equipment and resources

**Goals**:
- List department resources for student use
- Approve bookings based on prerequisites
- Track resource utilization for budgeting

**Pain Points**:
- Email-based booking requests are inefficient
- No centralized system for department resources
- Difficult to track who used what and when

**Tech Savviness**: Medium-High - uses technology daily but not cutting-edge

**Usage Scenario**:
Dr. Martinez creates listings for three lab instruments, setting availability rules and requiring approval. When students request bookings, she reviews their prerequisites and approves qualified requests. She generates monthly usage reports for her department head.

## Persona 3: Alex - Undergraduate Student

**Demographics**:
- Age: 19
- Role: Sophomore, Business major
- Tech usage: Primarily mobile

**Goals**:
- Find affordable/free campus resources
- Book equipment for class projects
- Connect with tutoring services

**Pain Points**:
- Doesn't know what resources exist on campus
- Missed opportunities due to lack of information
- Uncomfortable reaching out directly to departments

**Tech Savviness**: High mobile usage, prefers intuitive interfaces

**Usage Scenario**:
Alex's marketing class requires video production. He searches Campus Resource Hub for "video camera" and discovers multiple AV equipment options he didn't know existed. He books a camera kit, picks it up, and later leaves a positive review.

## Persona 4: Jennifer - System Administrator

**Demographics**:
- Age: 35
- Role: IT Administrator
- Oversees: Campus technology resources

**Goals**:
- Manage user accounts and permissions
- Moderate inappropriate content
- Generate usage analytics
- Ensure system security and integrity

**Pain Points**:
- Need visibility across all resources and bookings
- Must handle disputes and conflicts
- Compliance and audit requirements

**Tech Savviness**: Expert - IT professional

**Usage Scenario**:
Jennifer logs into the admin dashboard, reviews flagged content, approves several pending user registrations, resolves a booking conflict between two departments, and exports usage data for the quarterly IT governance meeting.

## Persona 5: Marcus - Event Organizer

**Demographics**:
- Age: 21
- Role: Student organization president
- Manages: Campus club events

**Goals**:
- Book event spaces for club meetings
- Find AV equipment for presentations
- Coordinate multiple resources for large events

**Pain Points**:
- Event planning requires booking multiple resources
- Uncertain availability far in advance
- Last-minute cancellations cause problems

**Tech Savviness**: Medium - uses productivity tools regularly

**Usage Scenario**:
Marcus is planning a 100-person conference. He books an auditorium, projector, sound system, and breakout rooms all through Campus Resource Hub. He communicates with multiple resource owners through the messaging system and successfully coordinates the event.
